PALAKKAD


Palakkad is located right in the middle of the state. Also, it is the largest district in Kerala since 2006. The district is nicknamed "the granary of Kerala". Palakkad is the gateway to Kerala due to the presence of the Palakkad Gap, in the Western Ghats. The 2,383 m high Anginda peak, which is situated in the border of Palakkad district, Nilgiris district, and Malappuram district, in Silent Valley National Park, is the highest point of elevation in Palakkad district.

TOP ATTRACTIONS


Nelliyampathy

The height of the hills ranges from 467 m to 1572 m. To reach Nelliyampathy, one has to take the road starting from Nenmara hat proceeds to the Pothundy Dam. The Pothundy Dam is a picturesque locale with facilities for boating. At certain places there are viewpoints from where the vast stretches of Palakkad district are visible . It also offers a splendid view of the Palakkad Gap.

Malampuzha Dam

Malampuzha Garden in Palakkad district is the only rock-cut garden in South India made by Nek Chand. The entire garden is made from broken pieces of bangles, tiles, used plastic cans, tins and other waste materials. Also situated in the gardens is the massive Malampuzha Yakshi. The flowering beds, fountains and rose gardens along with an aerial ropeway make it a place that instantly soothes your soul.

Silent Valley

Silent Valley National Park is a beautiful representation of the last remaining rain forest of Kerala. Silent Valley was declared as National Park in 1984. Initially there was only 89.52 sq.km. area under the Division which forms the core zone of the National Park. In 2007, an area of 148 sq.km. was added to this Division as buffer zone. In 1914, Silent Valley forests were declared as Reserved Forest.
